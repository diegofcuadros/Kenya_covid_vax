Our Vision

Addressing critical challenges at the intersection of environmental change, infectious disease, and human adaptation in Uganda

A Convergence of Expertise

The UC Uganda Research Center brings together three complementary fields of study to create a unique approach to global health challenges:

Genomics & Epigenetics

Understanding how environmental changes affect human biology at the molecular level

Infectious Disease Epidemiology

Tracking how viruses and pathogens spread through populations and respond to changing environments

Spatial Health Analytics

Using geographic mapping and AI to predict disease patterns and target interventions

Explore Our Research

Why Uganda?

A unique confluence of factors makes Uganda the ideal location for this initiative

Kampala: Central hub for research operations with major institutional partners
Eastern Region: Focus area for infectious disease research
Southwest Region: Batwa communities and environmental transition studies
Northern Uganda: Climate change and health adaptation research sites

Existing UC Footprint

Our three faculty members have established research programs across Uganda, providing immediate critical mass and operational foundation

Strong Partner Ecosystem

Dense network of high-capacity local partners (IDI, IDRC, Makerere University, UVRI) reduces start-up risk and accelerates impact

Epicenter of Research

Uganda sits at the intersection of HIV, malaria, and emerging-pathogen research, providing unparalleled training and grant opportunities

Environmental Diversity

Range of ecosystems and communities experiencing environmental transitions creates unique research opportunities

Key Ugandan Partners

Infectious Diseases Institute (IDI)

A major research center at Makerere University with operations across 92% of Uganda's districts

  • Expertise in HIV, TB, and emerging infections
  • State-of-the-art laboratories and outpatient clinic
  • Played key role in Uganda's COVID-19 and Ebola responses

Infectious Diseases Research Collaboration (IDRC)

A research institute with extensive malaria surveillance network across Uganda

  • Manages over 47 active research grants
  • Runs 70 malaria research centers across 15 districts
  • Developed AI-enabled surveillance systems

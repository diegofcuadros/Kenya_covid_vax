Our Vision

Addressing critical challenges at the intersection of environmental change, infectious disease, and human adaptation in Uganda

A Convergence of Expertise

The UC Uganda Research Center brings together three complementary fields of study to create a unique approach to global health challenges:

Genomics & Epigenetics

Understanding how environmental changes affect human biology at the molecular level

Infectious Disease Epidemiology

Tracking how viruses and pathogens spread through populations and respond to changing environments

Spatial Health Analytics

Using geographic mapping and AI to predict disease patterns and target interventions

Why Uganda?

A unique confluence of factors makes Uganda the ideal location for this initiative

Existing UC Footprint

Our three faculty members have established research programs across Uganda, providing immediate critical mass and operational foundation

Strong Partner Ecosystem

Dense network of high-capacity local partners (IDI, IDRC, Makerere University, UVRI) reduces start-up risk and accelerates impact

Epicenter of Research

Uganda sits at the intersection of HIV, malaria, and emerging-pathogen research, providing unparalleled training and grant opportunities

Environmental Diversity

Range of ecosystems and communities experiencing environmental transitions creates unique research opportunities

Key Ugandan Partners

Infectious Diseases Institute (IDI)

A major research center at Makerere University with operations across 92% of Uganda's districts

  • Expertise in HIV, TB, and emerging infections
  • State-of-the-art laboratories and outpatient clinic
  • Played key role in Uganda's COVID-19 and Ebola responses

Infectious Diseases Research Collaboration (IDRC)

A research institute with extensive malaria surveillance network across Uganda

  • Manages over 47 active research grants
  • Runs 70 malaria research centers across 15 districts
  • Developed AI-enabled surveillance systems

Center Structure

A robust organizational framework designed for sustainable impact

Organizational Design

The UC-URC will be established as a Ugandan-registered research entity with formal affiliations to UC and Makerere University, governed by a balanced Executive Board including representatives from both institutions and key stakeholders.

Governance Structure

Executive Board, Scientific Advisory Committee, and Community Advisory Boards ensure balanced representation

Leadership Model

Co-Directors (one UC-based, one Uganda-based) with Program Directors for each research pillar

Physical Infrastructure

Central Kampala headquarters with satellite research sites in regions with ongoing studies

Implementation Timeline

Phase 1: Establishment (Year 1)

Formalize partnerships, register legal entity, secure initial funding, and begin pilot research projects

Key Milestone: Partnership MOUs signed
Key Milestone: First research grants submitted

Phase 2: Growth (Years 2-3)

Expand research activities, launch training programs, develop data management systems, and strengthen community engagement

Key Milestone: First student exchanges begin
Key Milestone: Research publications from integrated projects

Phase 3: Expansion (Years 4-6)

Develop specialized research facilities, extend geographic reach, add new research domains, and build regional collaborations

Key Milestone: Dedicated research facilities operational
Key Milestone: Major center grants secured

Phase 4: Sustainability (Years 7-10)

Transition to majority Ugandan leadership, evaluate and refine strategic direction, and develop long-term funding mechanisms

Key Milestone: Majority Ugandan leadership team
Key Milestone: Sustainable funding model established

Dr. Kathleen Grogan

Research Lead

Genome-to-Population Health

This research pillar examines how environmental transitions affect human epigenomes and health outcomes, particularly in populations experiencing rapid lifestyle changes like the Batwa communities in Uganda.

Environmental Epigenetics

Studying how climate shifts, urbanization, and other environmental changes affect gene regulation at the molecular level

Biological Adaptation

Tracking how human populations adapt to changing environments through genetic and epigenetic mechanisms

Intergenerational Effects

Examining how environmental exposures may affect future generations through epigenetic inheritance

Co-infection Dynamics
